# 💫 About Me:
My name is Piet Zwane. I am not a computer science graduate. I do not have a university degree. I finished matric in South Africa in 2019 with results that closed that door — and I live with epilepsy, which has closed a few others along the way.<br><br>None of that stopped me.<br><br>I started working in hospitality — running technical support for a restaurant in Cape Town. I was the person they called when the point-of-sale system crashed on a Friday night, when the card machines went down, when the network dropped during a full house. I fixed hardware, maintained devices, trained staff on software, and learned something important: I am the kind of person who runs towards problems, not away from them.<br><br>That instinct led me to technology. Not through a university programme — through curiosity, discipline, and a lot of late nights. I started with the fundamentals: networking (TCP/IP, DNS, DHCP, VPN), Linux, Windows administration. Then I went deeper: cloud infrastructure on AWS — EC2, S3, IAM, VPC, Lambda, CloudFormation. Then deeper still: Docker, Kubernetes, Terraform, infrastructure as code.<br><br>Every single one of these skills was self-taught. No bootcamp. No degree. Just documentation, hands-on labs, and the stubborn belief that where you start does not have to define where you end up.<br><br>In February 2025, I passed the AWS Certified Cloud Practitioner exam. In October 2025, I earned the HackerRank Software Engineer certification. I am currently preparing for the AWS Solutions Architect – Associate exam. Each certification was a milestone I set for myself and hit on my own terms.<br><br>Today I work as an Operations Support Associate at Nampak Packaging Excellence in Johannesburg, managing operational workflows in a manufacturing environment. But my evenings and weekends belong to cloud-native security and Kubernetes. That is where I am building my future.<br><br>For this project, I set up a local K3s cluster from scratch, deployed Inspektor Gadget, ran live eBPF traces, and explored the Headlamp plugin architecture — all before writing a single word of this proposal. I watched kernel-level data stream across my terminal: syscalls, process trees, network connections, DNS lookups. Powerful data. And completely unusable for a security engineer at 2am without the right interface on top of it.<br><br>That is the problem I want to solve. Not because someone assigned it to me, but because I have spent the last five years teaching myself how systems work — and I have learned that the hardest problems are never about the technology. They are about making the technology usable for the humans who need it most.<br><br>I am not a Solutions Architect yet. But the journey from a matric certificate to running eBPF traces on a Kubernetes cluster was not given to me. I built it myself. And that same drive — that refusal to accept that closed doors are permanent — is exactly what I will bring to this project.
